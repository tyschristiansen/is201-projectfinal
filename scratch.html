Lake Powell Notes

Wake Surfing at Lake Powell

Lake Powell is the kind of place where red canyon walls, glassy water, and a clean boat wake can turn a normal summer day into a story people keep retelling.

Plan the Ride

Why Powell Feels Different

Surfing behind a boat is already fun, but Lake Powell adds the setting: narrow canyons, sandstone shelves, warm light, and long stretches of open water. The best rides feel calm and fast at the same time.

Play Surfing Powell

Lake Powell gives wake surfing a backdrop that looks unreal even before the rope drops.

Simple Game Plan

  1. Pick the right water.
    • Look for calmer coves away from heavy boat traffic.
    • Ride early or late when the wind is softer.
    • Keep plenty of space from canyon walls and other boats.
  2. Set up the boat.
    • Use ballast and speed settings that create a clean pocket.
    • Check the rider, rope, flag, and spotter before each pull.
    • Keep music and conversation low enough for clear signals.
  3. Ride with control.
    • Stay relaxed through the start.
    • Shift weight gently instead of making huge corrections.
    • Celebrate small progress, especially the first ropeless ride.

Favorite Part

The best moment is when the rope goes slack and the board settles into the wave. For a few seconds, everything gets simple: balance, water, boat, canyon, and the small feeling that you might actually have this.
